Copywrong

When someone makes a copy of an intellectual work, the original is completely unaffected. The original owner still has their intellectual property and are free to use it just as they did before a copy was made.

This act, of creating a copy, is completely innocent and should not be criminalized. The original owner of the work still has their copy. No theft has taken place.

Thus, the act of "Piracy" (Copywronger propaganda term) is completely innocent.

However, there is a way that intellectual property can be genuinely stolen:

If a man destroys, damages, or hinders another man's copy of a work, they have stolen it.
Before the act, they were able to enjoy their work in peace. After the act, the work was removed.

The reality of "Copyright law" is that not only does it criminalize the completely innocent act of copying,
but it legalizes genuine theft.

If a person possesses a copy of a work which was obtained in a fashion not approved by the "Copyright holder" then the Copyright holder is legally empowered to remove that person's ability to enjoy the work.

Even more heinously, should any kind and thoughtful person create a copy of the work they are enjoying a friend, the "Copyright holder" can take them to court and ruin their life... for being nice!

These draconian, wicked, exploitative laws are made so that evil people can make more money from making intellectual work, because after they make it, they can steal from people who make "illegal copies".

The common defense for these wicked laws is that without them, there would be "no way" for people to make money writing books, video games, TV shows, etcetera. Confront a copyright advocate, and you will likely be ridiculed as a hypocrite, that "none of your favorite X would exist without copyright" (Where X is some category of intellectual work you enjoy.

However, this is not only demonstrably false, but totally reprehensible from the onset.

To demonstrate how ridiculous this is, imagine if someone decided to pass a law saying that chair manufacturers could sue you for sitting wrong. This ridiculous law would then be justified by saying that nobody would ever bother making chairs if we didn't have them, and that the fact you enjoy chairs proves you're actually a hypocrite.

Not only is it totally incorrect (People have been making chairs for hundreds if not thousands of years without exploitative evil chair regulation laws) but the whole basis for such an idea is purely evil- that a person should "own" the right to control other peoples' lives.

But doesn't copyright protect creators from having their work stolen?

As discussed before, piracy is not theft. If someone "steals" your idea, you still have your copy of the idea. You don't suddenly "not have it anymore". Furthermore, Copyright may actually be (ab)used to steal YOUR WORK from you.

If someone else just-so-happens to have come up with an idea that's "too similar" to yours, they can claim that you are violating their copyright and demand you shut down your operation.

This sort of thing happens all the time and is especially prevalent in patent law.

The brutal reality of copyright is that It only helps people with large legal teams.

If you are a small independent creative laborer, it is not only a legitimate fear, but a likely outcome that if your work is "too good" that someone will steal it from you. Not just pirate it.

I, David Webster, live in fear that some big corporation with tons of lawyers will come and steal my work from me, claiming I've stepped on their patent or used their API or whatever. Copyright is what empowers sinners to do this. I pray that the Lord Jesus Christ protect me from such wicked men.

Possible Copywrong Errors

Most Beloved, I have labored tirelessly to save you from the demonic influences of copywrong, however I am but one man, and the forces of evil are seemingly everywhere. So I acknowledge that I may have genuinely violated some portion of these wicked, evil laws. I predict or know I have done so in one of these fashions:

  1. I may have left compiled binaries in the source tree, and I believe there are some in the git history.
    These compiled binaries may contain static or dynamic linkages to copywrong'd software such as SDL2 and GCC.
  2. I used the C programming language and C standard library to write the Seabass metacompiler and its
  3. I used copyrighted OpenGL and OpenAL documentation to create the OpenGL and OpenAL wrappers.
    the function prototypes were converted almost verbatim.
  4. SDL2 was used to implement `fun.hbas` and `funbas.hbas`. SDL2 itself is not included in the source code, however SDL2 is directly called. SDL2 does not require any form of attribution however it does worryingly say that it requires you may not misrepresent the origins of the software. Granted that I include none of SDL2 itself, this should not be an issue.
  5. A snippet of (most likely copyrighted) code was converted by-hand to implement `glPerspective` in `gl1_1.hbas`. This code was originally found on `nehe`'s game dev website, but I also found out Khronos provided the same code.
  6. The likely trademarked names __WIN32__ and __APPLE__ appear in the source code of the Seabass metacompiler.
  7. The name "GNU" Likely appears somewhere, and gcc/clang/tinycc are each mentioned by name.

These admissions are here for your benefit, beloved, so that you may be aware of possible persecution by the state should your copy be considered "illegal"
